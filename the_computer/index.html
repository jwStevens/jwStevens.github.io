The Computer

An artistic tool

Many people believe that computers are machines that are too complex to be considered an art. There are also people who embrace computers in many ways to enhance their art. The way we will use a computer is for the creation of digital content, then strapped together with code.

The Absolute Basics

This section is considered the absolute minimum of understanding to following this book; if you have trouble grasping these, you’re going ot have a hard time.

The Keyboard / Mouse

This is your form of input. These two devices shouldn’t require too much explaining, however learning proper typing would aid you, albeit not needed.

I will not refer to the windows button as “The Windows Button” and from here on will be denoted as “The Super Key” or “super”. This is for my own insanity.

You will require the keyboard as shortcuts are in every software and will help you work muich faster than with just a mouse or on some touch device.

The keys you may not know that aren’t the letters and numbers are:

  • Control / ctrl
  • Alt / alt
  • Shift / shift
  • F1-F12
  • Backspace vs Delete
  • Forward Slash, Backward Slash, Pipe
  • Math symbols: + - * /

A standard mouse will have 3 buttons:

  • Left Mouse Button / LMB
  • Middle Mouse Button / MMB
  • Right Mouse Button / RMB

You also need to know how to make folders and navigate in a file browser, I will teach you how to use a terminal if you wish, you’ll be a lot faster this way.

The Monitor / Screen

The monitor is the most important feedback for a computer user. As a game developer it is key to note what resolution your monitor is. A standard monitor is 1920x1080.

A monitor operates differently than a cartesian graph, in that downwards (to the bottom edge), is positive Y.

If you don’t understand things like resolution, cartesian graphs or pixel; checkout the Understanding Coordinate Systems. You don’t need to read this now, but if you can manage, it would help.
