Creating Your First Game (Draft)

Okay.

This is your first game.

Don’t go crazy with it, but pick something that sounds kind of cool.

Whatever you choose, stick with it. It will pay off.

Conclusion

The point of looking at these was to show you that everyone had a secondary goal

Chosing your first game

So, hopefully you’ve thought of something you want to make, as well as you’ve also picked some sort of secondary goal beside having a game to play; for example a long-term game or learning-project.

The Process

Step 1: Write down your idea in one sentence.

Dreamlight is a reaction-time based game about facing a lurking evil.

Dark Magic Defender is a tower defence game with custom towers and enemies.

Space Attack is a galaga clone.

These were all “Essences” of what the developer wanted at the moment of inspiration. Capture it now as it may come and go at points, and it will be nice to have some phrase to return to.

Step 2: Choose a starting point

Read step 3 before leaving this page though!

There are a million places to start!

A game is a combination of many art forms and your idea you created in step one should be attached to some inspiration about what you want this game to be; you thought of one of the characters, you pictured a level, you know how you want the game to play etc.

I say that is the place to start. Find that intially inspiring place, and begin there.
